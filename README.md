# Google Review Response Templates

Copy-paste response templates for every type of Google review: positive, negative, mixed, fake, and star-only. Written for local business owners who want to reply fast without sounding like a robot.

## Why reply at all?

Google has confirmed that responding to reviews improves your local SEO. Beyond rankings, 89% of consumers read business responses to reviews. Your reply isn't for the reviewer — it's for the next customer reading it.

## How to use these

Find the review type below, copy the template, and swap the [brackets] with real details from the review. Always reference something specific they said — generic replies are worse than no reply.

## 5-star reviews

**Specific detail:** *"Thanks so much, [name]! Glad the [specific thing they mentioned] hit the mark. See you next time!"*

**Team shoutout:** *"[Name], thank you! I'll pass this along to [staff member] — they'll be thrilled you noticed. Hope to see you again soon."*

**New customer:** *"Welcome, [name] — happy your first visit went well! Thanks for giving us a shot, and see you again soon."*

## 4-star reviews

**Thank + invite feedback:** *"Thanks for the review, [name]! Glad [specific positive] worked out. If there's anything that would've made it 5 stars, we'd love to hear it — [email]."*

**Acknowledge the gap:** *"Appreciate the honest review, [name]. We hear you on [the minor issue] — working on it. Thanks for sticking with us."*

## 3-star reviews (mixed)

**Own both halves:** *"Thanks for the balanced feedback, [name]. Glad [positive] worked, and you're right that [negative] needs fixing. We've [action taken]. Hope we can earn those last two stars next visit."*

## 1-2 star reviews (negative)

The rules: respond within 24-48 hours, never argue, never get defensive, take it offline.

**Service failure:** *"[Name], I'm sorry — this isn't the experience we want anyone to have. You're right that [specific issue] shouldn't have happened. I'd like to make it right: please reach me directly at [email/phone]. — [Owner name]"*

**Misunderstanding (correct gently, never argue):** *"Thanks for the feedback, [name]. I'm sorry for the frustration. For context, [brief factual clarification — one sentence max]. Either way, we clearly didn't communicate well, and I'd like to fix that — [email/phone]."*

**You genuinely messed up:** *"No excuses, [name] — we dropped the ball on [issue]. I've [concrete fix made]. If you're willing to give us another chance, [offer]. Reach me at [email/phone]. — [Owner name]"*

## Star-only reviews (no text)

**5 stars, no text:** *"Thanks for the 5 stars, [name]! Come back and see us soon."*

**1 star, no text:** *"Hi [name], I'm sorry to see the 1 star. I can't find your visit in our records — I'd like to understand what went wrong. Please reach me at [email/phone]. — [Owner name]"*

## Suspected fake reviews

Reply publicly first (the next reader can't tell it's fake), then report it to Google.

**Template:** *"Hi [name], we take every review seriously, but we have no record of your visit. If you're a real customer, please contact us at [email/phone] so we can address this. If this review was left in error, we'd appreciate it being removed."*

Then flag the review in your Google Business Profile under "Report review."

## Tone tips

Keep it to 2-4 sentences — longer looks defensive. Use their name and one specific detail from their review. Sign negative-review replies with a real name; "Owner" reads corporate. And never copy-paste the same reply twice in a row — Google's spam filters and your customers both notice.
